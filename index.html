Background & Questions

Background

Music, essentially, is who we are (Philpott & Plummeridge, 2001). Our group is curious about what people listen to with Spotify. It is possible that if this project reviews Spotify data over time, we will be able to see changes of people’s taste in music.

Questions

The most popular artists on Spotify in 2016 are Drake, Justin Bieber, Rihanna, Kanye West and Twenty One Pilots. We want to know what they have in common and what features in their songs may have contributed to their success. Moving into 2017, we wonder if the new releases continue the trend from 2016 or if there are emerging new genres.

Citation

Philpott, C., & Plummeridge, C. (2001). Issues in music teaching. Pg. 48 - 51. London: Routledge/Falmer.

Data Collection & Analysis

Data Collection

In the process of collecting data, we used the native Spotify REST API to extract artist data, track data and track feature data, new releases and other related artists data.

We also used a Python package, Spotipy, when working with authentication. It provided us a way to seamlessly manage credentials in a project that heavily relies on collaboration.

Analysis Method

We researched Spotify’s 5 most popular artists of 2016 (Spotify, 2017). We extracted data of their top tracks and the respective features. Then we branched out our search to include the related artists of the most popular artists to create a network of artists to induce a wider range of diversity in our analysis. A diverse dataset is useful to help produce more accurate results. On the other hand, all these artists share much in common to be connected to each other. This prevents too much anomaly to a degree.

Spotify provides a fairly clean dataset pulled from its API in JSON format. There is minimal data cleaning needed for this project.

For the data analysis process, we used the Pandas, JSON, Matplotlib and Collections packages to unpack the JSON files and start to explore the variables and their relations with each other. This allows us to create visualizations with targeted variables based on the relations.

Citation

Spotify. (2016, December 07). Drake is Spotify's Most Streamed Artist for 2016. Retrieved June 04, 2017, from https://news.spotify.com/us/2016/12/01/wrapped-2016/

Limitations & Ethics

Limitations

Spotify API offers a vast amount of data from artists, tracks, users, playlists, each with very detailed granularity. We only explored the artists and their immediate related artists and tracks. We didn’t take location or specific user habits into consideration, which is a missed opportunity to analyze how and why certain artists and tracks appeal to users. The statistical concepts in our analysis are more descriptive than predictive. This leaves room for more statistical exploration.

Ethics Consideration

We only extracted data obtainable with or without a basic Spotify developer account. No user data of any sort was collected. We consider this data collection and analysis to be very low risk with few ethical implications.

Visuals

The visualization tool of choice is Tableau for its versatile ability to create graphs and tell a story.
